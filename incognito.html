The Incognito Mode Myth: What It Really Does

Myth: "Incognito mode makes you completely anonymous online."

Reality: Incognito or private browsing mode does not make you invisible. It simply prevents your browser from saving your history, cookies, and form data locally.

Here’s What Incognito Mode Actually Does:
  • Doesn't save browsing history, cookies, or search entries on your device.
  • Helps you sign into multiple accounts in the same browser.
  • Prevents local tracking — but not from your ISP, websites, or your employer/school network.
What It Doesn’t Do:
  • Doesn't hide your IP address or location.
  • Doesn’t protect you from malware or phishing.
  • Doesn’t stop websites from tracking you via fingerprinting.

Pro Tip: Use a VPN, secure browsers (like Brave or Tor), and privacy-focused search engines (like DuckDuckGo) for real online anonymity.
